Why Limit the Residual Stream to Layers and Not Tokens?

Persistent Memory for Continuous Latent Reasoning. We find that latent-reasoning LLMs forget what they just figured out — and fix it with three tiny learned gates and a memory stream that costs just 1.41% extra parameters.

Mujtaba FarhanAlgoverse AI Research
Maheep ChaudharyIndependent

TL;DR

The paper in three bites

🕳️

The Concept Bottleneck

In latent reasoning (CoCoNuT), each reasoning pass overwrites the previous hidden state. Facts computed early are lost by the time the answer is generated — 87% of pass-1 information is gone by pass 6.

🚪

Three Learned Gates

AGCLR adds a persistent concept stream — a residual memory across passes — controlled by read, forget, and write gates. Like an LSTM, but gating memory across reasoning passes, not timesteps.

📈

Compounding Gains

+2.6% on GSM8K, +3.6% EM on HotpotQA, +4.0% on ProsQA over vanilla CoCoNuT — and the gap grows with reasoning depth, exactly where the bottleneck bites hardest.

The Method

AGCLR: a memory stream with three doors 🚪🚪🚪

At every latent token position, a persistent vector ct (the concept stream) rides alongside the hidden state ht. Three sigmoid gates decide what gets remembered, retrieved, and pruned. Click a gate to learn what it does.

👆 Click a gate

Each gate is a learned sigmoid over the layer-normed hidden state — e.g. rt = σ(Wr ĥt). Together they cost only 1.41% extra parameters on GPT-2.

The full update, four lines 🧮

1ĥₜ = LayerNorm(hₜ)normalize the current thought
2rₜ, fₜ, wₜ = σ(W·ĥₜ)three gates, each ∈ [0,1]ᵈ
3h′ₜ = (1−fₜ)⊙hₜ + rₜ⊙cₜ₋₁prune noise, pull in memory
4cₜ = LayerNorm(cₜ₋₁ + wₜ⊙h′ₜ)commit to the stream

Results

Numbers that compound 📊

All methods use the same GPT-2 (117M) base. Pick a dataset — bars animate to the paper's Table 1. Hover a bar for details.

🧠 Memory retention across passes

Cosine similarity of each pass's hidden state to pass 1 (100 validation samples, epoch 15). Vanilla decays monotonically to 0.126 (≈87% information loss); AGCLR stabilizes at 0.216 — retaining 71% more information at final generation.

📉 Depth helps us, hurts them

On ProsQA, vanilla CoCoNuT peaks at 95% at curriculum stage 5, then degrades to 92% at stage 6 (fully latent). AGCLR keeps climbing to 96% — the concept bottleneck, resolved.

Ablations

Memory, not parameters 🧪

Is it just extra weights? No. Freezing the write gate after pass 2 costs only −0.8% EM — the stream is persistent storage, not a scratchpad: write early, read often. And every gate earns its keep (HotpotQA EM).

⚖️ Honest limitations

Single-seed runs on GPT-2 124M across three benchmarks; scaling to 1B+ models, multi-seed variance estimates, and broader benchmarks (MuSiQue, 2WikiMultihopQA, StrategyQA) are future work. The architecture itself is parameter-agnostic. Total training: ~8 hours on a single A10 GPU.
